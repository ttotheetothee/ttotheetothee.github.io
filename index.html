About

The person
behind the work.

I've always been drawn to problems that don't have obvious answers — the kind where you have to sit with messy data, strip away the noise, and build a clear picture from scratch. That instinct showed up during my time at EY, where I learned quickly that the real value wasn't the model itself — it was the question underneath it. Why is this borrower flagged? What's the signal the number isn't showing? That's the work I kept coming back to.

What draws me to strategy consulting is the pace of problems. Every engagement is a new industry, a new set of constraints, and a short window to build conviction. I think best under that kind of pressure — when the analysis has to become a recommendation someone can act on before the meeting ends. I structure first, dig into the data second, and communicate in a way that makes the answer feel inevitable.

Away from the desk, I'm competitive in ways that tend to bleed into the work. Golf has taught me that discipline in process is the only path to consistent outcomes — you can't force the shot. I also play competitive FPS at a high level, which has sharpened the part of my brain that reads patterns fast and adapts under pressure. Both have made me a better analyst: patient in preparation, decisive in execution. I spend a lot of time outdoors, which is where I do most of my clearest thinking.
