Executive narrative

Superstore is a growing business with a margin problem. Sales have increased steadily from $484K in 2014 to $733K in 2017, yet overall margin remains compressed at 12.47%. The primary driver of margin erosion is the Furniture category, operating at just 2.5% margin — seven times below its peers — largely due to Tables running at -8.6% margin on $207K of sales. Simultaneously, Binders in Office Supplies are being discounted at 37%, surrendering profit on a product that remains viable even at lower discount rates. The West region is the clear growth engine, expanding from 30.5% to 34.1% share. The business requires an immediate pricing and discount strategy review before sustained growth masks the underlying margin erosion.

Findings & recommendations

Critical

Tables operating at -8.6% margin on $207K in sales

Highest discount rate across all sub-categories at 26%. Every unit sold generates a net loss — at this volume, the drag on overall profitability is material.

Conduct immediate pricing review on Tables. Evaluate whether the discount policy is commercially justified or a structural pricing error.

Critical

Bookcases operating at -3.0% margin

Secondary loss-maker compounding Furniture's overall 2.5% margin — category is running 7x below Technology and Office Supplies peers.

Bundle pricing review alongside Tables. Assess whether discontinuation or repricing delivers greater margin recovery.

Monitor

Binders discounted at 37% average — highest in Office Supplies

Product remains profitable at 14.9% margin despite heavy discounting. Profit is being surrendered on a high-volume product unnecessarily.

Reduce Binders discount incrementally. A 10-point reduction could materially improve Office Supplies margin with limited volume risk.

Opportunity

West region growing from 30.5% to 34.1% share over four years

Strongest and most consistent growth trajectory across all four regions. Represents the business's primary organic growth engine.

Prioritize inventory, marketing, and sales resource allocation toward the West region to sustain and accelerate momentum.

Investigate

South Technology volume collapsed 68% in 2015, margin preserved

Revenue compression without margin deterioration signals a volume problem, not an efficiency problem. Root cause remains unresolved.

Investigate whether the collapse was structural or situational before drawing strategic conclusions about the South region.
